Unsupervised 3D Learning in the Wild

Unsupervised understanding of the 3D world is an emerging topic in Computer Vision that has been gaining popularity recently. The workshop will cover both, current methods as well as next steps in unsupervised and weakly-supervised 3D learning. We have compiled a list of topics that we will ask the speakers to cover:

  • Unsupervised and weakly supervised 3D learning
  • Learning of shape, appearance, lighting, shading and material properties
  • Learning from in-the-wild images and videos
  • Priors on shape, shading, category, deformation or motion
  • Differentiable rendering

Beyond these topics the workshop will shed light on the next steps of the field. For example:

  • How to evaluate methods trained on in-the-wild data, as ground truth information is usually unavailable?
  • How can we deal with dataset bias and generalization?
  • Current models are often low resolution due to memory and compute constraints - can we make them more efficient?
  • How to move beyond single-category learning to more generalized 3D understanding?
  • How to recover complex appearance properties beyond geometry?

The workshop will be held online in conjuction with ICCV 2021. The talks will be livestreamed and recorded on Youtube and the conference platform. There are no papers at this workshop.
